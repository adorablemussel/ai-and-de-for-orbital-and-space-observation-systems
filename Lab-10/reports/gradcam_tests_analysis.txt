GRAD-CAM ANALYSIS
================
Experiment 1: Forest
Prediction: forest
Confidence: 0.9636
Observed attention: Blurry, diffuse attention on random vegetation patches.
Interpretation: Model relies on homogenous textural patterns rather than explicit objects.

Experiment 2: Residential
Prediction: residential
Confidence: 0.9960
Observed attention: Highly localized on geometric grid patterns, roads, and buildings.
Interpretation: Model correctly captures distinct urban structural elements.

Experiment 3: Highway
Prediction: river
Confidence: 0.8113
Observed attention: Focused tightly along the linear path of the highway lane.
Interpretation: Dataset bias error. Lacking a 'highway' class, the model misclassifies the linear geometry as a 'river'.

Experiment 4: Noise
Prediction: forest
Confidence: 0.6333
Observed attention: Spurious and random hot spots across the image.
Interpretation: Forced prediction where the model mistakenly matches random pixel variance with forest texture.

Final Conclusions:
- The model does not always focus on meaningful regions; for the river sample, it targeted the shore infrastructure intersection instead of the water.
- Grad-CAM successfully uncovers critical model vulnerabilities, such as shortcut learning and domain errors.
- Unknown inputs produce high-confidence false predictions, indicating a lack of safe rejection mechanisms.
- Explainability does not guarantee correctness but serves as an indispensable debugging tool.
- XAI is vital in Earth Observation to prevent silent, disastrous failures caused by distribution shifts or sensor noise.
